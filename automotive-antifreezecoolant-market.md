<p><p>The automotive antifreeze/coolant market consists of several types, primarily including ethylene glycol, propylene glycol, and glycerin. Ethylene glycol is the most widely used due to its efficient heat transfer and freezing point depression properties. Propylene glycol serves as a non-toxic alternative, suitable for environments where contact with food or animals is possible. Glycerin, a less common option, is biodegradable and offers low toxicity but may provide less effective cooling. Additional formulations and additives also fall under the "others" category, enhancing performance and protection.</p></p>
