<p><p>The long-grain rice seed market primarily comprises two types: Japonica rice and Indica rice. Japonica rice, known for its short, plump grains, is typically grown in cooler climates and has a sticky texture when cooked, favored in East Asian cuisines. Indica rice, characterized by long, slender grains, is more abundant in tropical regions and is less sticky, making it ideal for dishes like biryani and fried rice. Both types cater to diverse culinary preferences and nutritional requirements globally.</p></p>
