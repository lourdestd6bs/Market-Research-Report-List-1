<p><p>The inulin and fructooligosaccharide (FOS) market comprises three main types: Native inulin, FOS derived from sugar, and FOS from inulin. Native inulin is extracted from plants like chicory and is valued for its prebiotic properties. FOS from sugar is produced through the hydrolysis of sucrose, often utilized in food and beverages for its sweetness and functional benefits. FOS from inulin is derived from the enzymatic breakdown of inulin, providing a similar prebiotic benefit and enhancing dietary fiber content in products.</p></p>
